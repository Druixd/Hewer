# THREE UNIQUE HYBRID-CASUAL PUZZLE CONCEPTS

Unexplored mechanics. Zero direct competitors in mobile.

---

## Concept 1: THERMOMORPH - "Heat & Shape"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Core Mechanic** | Tap a shape to heat it, which makes it expand. Tap again to cool it, which makes it shrink. Use expansion and shrinkage to fit shapes through gaps, bridge connections, or open pathways. Fill all target zones to complete the level. |
| **One sentence** | "Heat it to grow, cool it to shrink, fit it through." |
| **ASMR elements** | Metal ticking as it cools, faint heat shimmer sound on expansion, satisfying snap when a gap closes perfectly, soft metallic chime on level complete. |
| **Why it's never been done** | Thermal expansion has appeared as a side-effect in physics sandboxes, but not as the framing puzzle mechanic in a mobile game. |
| **Mechanic to scale** | 2 shapes to 20 shapes, precision timing, shapes that must grow just enough, and multiple colors requiring different heat levels. |
| **Art** | Geometric metal shapes. Hot shapes get a warm orange glow. Cold shapes get a blue tint. Flat 2D, with glow handled as a filter or effect. |
| **3-sec ad understanding** | Screen: red shape is tapped and grows. Green shape is tapped and shrinks. Red fits through the wide gap. Text: "Heat to grow. Cool to shrink. Beat the level." |
| **Ease of dev** | Very easy. Resize sprite on tap. No physics. No pathfinding. Switch color overlay on tap. One core input. |

## Concept 2: PHASE SHIFT - "Change State"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Core Mechanic** | Tap an object to cycle its state: ice to water to steam. Each state has different behavior. Ice is solid, blocks paths, and can be pushed. Water flows down and fills containers. Steam rises and floats upward. Use state changes to navigate obstacles and reach the goal. |
| **One sentence** | "Freeze it to block. Boil it to rise. What goes up must come back down." |
| **ASMR elements** | Water drip and hiss when boiling, crisp ice cracking on freeze, soft steam hiss, satisfying plop when water lands. |
| **Why it's never been done** | Phase changes appear in educational games and obscure PC puzzle games, but not as a simplified one-tap hybrid-casual mobile loop. |
| **Art** | Flat blobs with texture cues: ice has frosted texture, water is smooth blue, steam is wispy and translucent. Same base shape, three visual modes. |
| **3-sec ad** | Ice blob freezes and blocks a gate, tap turns it into water that flows through, tap turns it into steam that rises over the obstacle. Text: "Freeze. Flow. Rise. Solve it." |
| **Ease of dev** | Easy to moderate. Three states on the same sprite, with three behavior modes. Can be built with authored paths and conditionals before real fluid physics. |

## Concept 3: SURFACE TENSION - "Float & Connect"

| | |
|---|---|
| **Core Mechanic** | Color-coded objects float on a water surface. Drag one object and surface tension pulls a chain of other same-color objects. Pull the group to a destination zone. Pull too hard and the chain breaks. Connect all colored objects to matching zones to complete the level. |
| **One sentence** | "Drag to pull, chain to connect, break to lose." |
| **ASMR elements** | Water plip when an object drops, gentle tinkling as the chain tightens, satisfying snap when the chain connects to a destination, soft surface ripple on reset. |
| **Why it's never been done** | Water surface physics appear in toy apps and browser demos, but the tension-chain mechanic has not been framed as the core of a mobile puzzle game. |
| **Art** | Colored circles floating on a blue water surface. Connection strings are thin glowing lines between linked objects. Minimalist and readable. |
| **3-sec ad** | Blue circle is dragged, three blue circles trail behind on elastic tethers, the chain lands in a blue target zone. Text: "Drag. Chain. Solve." |
| **Ease of dev** | Easy to moderate. Sprite array, linear tether between objects, and a distance threshold for breaking. |
